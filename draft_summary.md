# Executive Summary Report for Xiaomi

## Executive Summary
Xiaomi Corporation experienced a period of significant strategic and financial evolution from mid-2025 to mid-2026. The company achieved record financial performance in the first half and full year of 2025, driven primarily by the rapid scaling and profitability milestone of its Smart Electric Vehicle (EV) segment, alongside robust growth in IoT and Internet Services. However, the momentum faced headwinds in Q1 2026, with notable declines in revenue and profit due to macroeconomic pressures, rising component costs, and intensified market competition.

A dominant theme throughout the period is aggressive capital management. Xiaomi executed substantial share buyback programs, culminating in a HK$20 billion repurchase plan announced in May 2026, supported by an automatic repurchase mechanism. These buybacks necessitated continuous adjustments to the company's Weighted Voting Rights (WVR) structure to maintain regulatory compliance. Corporate governance also saw significant updates, including VIE restructuring and the adoption of new Articles of Association to facilitate virtual shareholder meetings. Key risks remain centered on ongoing regulatory disputes in India and margin compression in the smartphone and EV segments.

## Detailed Findings

### 1. Financial Performance and Segment Analysis
Xiaomi's financial trajectory shifted from robust growth in 2025 to contraction in early 2026.

*   **H1 2025 & Q3 2025 Records:** Total revenue for H1 2025 reached RMB 227,249.4 million (+38.2% YoY), with profit rising 146.3% to RMB 22,765.9 million. Q3 2025 saw revenue of RMB 113,120.7 million (+22.3% YoY) and adjusted net profit of RMB 11,310.9 million (+80.9%). The Smart EV segment achieved its first quarterly operating profit of RMB 700 million in Q3 2025. (截至2025年6月30日止三個月及六個月之業績公告, 2025-08-19; 截至2025年9月30日止三個月及九個月之業績公告, 2025-11-18)
*   **FY 2025 Full Year:** Revenue hit RMB 457,286.7 million (+25.0%), and profit was RMB 41,566.4 million (+76.3%). The Smart EV segment achieved a positive annual operating profit of RMB 900 million, delivering 411,082 vehicles. (截至2025年12月31日止年度之全年業績公告, 2026-03-24)
*   **Q1 2026 Slowdown:** Revenue fell 10.9% YoY to RMB 99,141.6 million, and profit dropped 56.5% to RMB 4,734.6 million. The Smart EV segment recorded an operating loss of RMB 3.1 billion, and gross margin decreased to 20.1% due to lower SU7 Ultra delivery proportion, vehicle purchase tax subsidies, and rising core component prices. (截至2026年3月31日止三個月之業績公告, 2026-05-26)
*   **Smartphone Segment:** Faced margin pressure throughout the period. H1 2025 gross margin fell to 12.0% from 13.5% due to overseas competition. Q1 2026 saw revenue drop 12.5% YoY and margin fall to 10.1% due to rising component (storage) costs and competition, despite a record high Average Selling Price (ASP). (截至2025年6月30日止三個月及六個月之業績公告, 2025-08-19; 截至2026年3月31日止三個月之業績公告, 2026-05-26)
*   **IoT Segment:** Revenue hit a record in H1 2025 (RMB 71,051.4 million) but fell 23.7% YoY in Q1 2026, explicitly attributed to the reduction of national subsidies in mainland China. (截至2025年6月30日止三個月及六個月之業績公告, 2025-08-19; 截至2026年3月31日止三個月之業績公告, 2026-05-26)

### 2. Capital Management and Share Capital Structure
The company aggressively managed its capital structure through buybacks and incentive schemes, requiring continuous adjustments to its WVR framework.

*   **Share Buyback Programs:** Xiaomi engaged in continuous share repurchases throughout the period, detailed in numerous Next Day Disclosure Returns (e.g., 翌日披露報表, 2025-09-29; 翌日披露報表, 2025-10-08). On May 26, 2026, the Board adopted a new HK$20 billion share buyback plan, effective June 2, 2026. (自願性公告 - 於市場實施200億港元股份購回計劃, 2026-05-26). On June 18, 2026, an Automatic Share Repurchase Plan (capped at HK$4 billion) was implemented to allow systematic buybacks even during restricted periods. (自動股份購回計劃公告, 2026-06-18)
*   **Share Cancellations:** Repurchased shares were consistently designated for cancellation rather than treasury. Major cancellations included 215,333,400 shares in Nov 2025, 36,747,800 in Apr 2026, 84,002,600 in May 2026, and 57,810,200 in June 2026. (翌日披露報表, 2025-11-12; 翌日披露報表, 2026-04-14; 翌日披露報表, 2026-05-19; 翌日披露報表, 2026-06-25)
*   **WVR Adjustments:** To comply with Listing Rules 8A.13 and 8A.15 (ensuring WVR proportion does not increase post-cancellation), the company converted Class A shares to Class B shares on a one-to-one basis following cancellations. Examples include conversions of 4,728,665 shares (Nov 2025), 6,339,850 shares (Apr 2026), 14,478,942 shares (May 2026), and 9,962,677 shares (June 2026). (翌日披露報表, 2025-11-12; 翌日披露報表, 2026-04-14; 翌日披露報表, 2026-05-19; 翌日披露報表, 2026-06-25)
*   **Employee Incentive Issuances:** The company frequently issued new Class B shares under various schemes (Pre-IPO, Post-IPO, 2023 Share Plan). For instance, 44,236,295 award shares were granted in Aug 2025, and 52,522,873 in May 2026. (根據2023年股份計劃授出獎勵及根據2024年小米香港股份計劃授出小米香港購股權, 2025-08-20; 根據2023年股份計劃授出獎勵, 2026-05-27)

### 3. Corporate Governance and Structural Updates
Significant updates were made to corporate governance documents and the VIE structure.

*   **VIE Restructuring:** On April 2, 2026, the company announced the termination of existing VIE contractual arrangements and the establishment of new ones. This involved restructuring the registered shareholders of four key Domestic Holding Companies (e.g., Xiaomi Tech, Beijing Wali Network) to improve administrative efficiency, resulting in the exit of former shareholders (like co-founders Hong Feng and Li Wanqiang) and the addition of new employee shareholders. (持續關連交易終止現有合約安排及訂立新合約安排, 2026-04-02)
*   **Articles of Association Update:** A special resolution was passed on June 2, 2026, to adopt the 19th Amended and Restated Memorandum and Articles of Association. Key updates included provisions for virtual shareholder meetings and electronic voting, and updated Terms of Reference for the Nomination Committee. (建議採納本公司的新組織章程大綱及細則, 2026-04-28; 經修訂和重述組織章程大綱及細則, 2026-06-02)
*   **AGM Results:** At the June 2, 2026 AGM, all resolutions were passed, including the HK$20B buyback mandate and the new M&A. However, there was significant B-class shareholder opposition (~36-37%) regarding the share issuance mandate and ~24% opposition regarding the re-appointment of the auditor. (2026年6月2日股東週年大會投票結果, 2026-06-02)
*   **Insider Dealings:** Vice Chairman Lin Bin announced a share reduction plan in Dec 2025 to sell up to USD 2 billion in Class B shares over several years to establish an investment fund. Conversely, Chairman Lei Jun increased his shareholding in Nov 2025. (自願公告, 2025-12-28; 自願公告 - 控股股東增持本公司股份, 2025-11-24)

### 4. Risk Factors
*   **India Regulatory Risk:** Xiaomi Technology India Private Limited remains under investigation by Indian authorities. As of March 31, 2026, bank accounts with INR 48,883,184,000 (approx. RMB 3.58 billion) remain restricted. Management maintains they have valid defenses and has not made significant provisions. (截至2026年3月31日止三個月之業績公告, 2026-05-26)
*   **Margin Compression:** The company explicitly cited "intensified competition" and "rising core component (storage) prices" as reasons for the decline in smartphone gross margins (falling to 10.1% in Q1 2026) and EV margins (falling to 20.1% in Q1 2026). (截至2026年3月31日止三個月之業績公告, 2026-05-26)
*   **Subsidy Dependency:** The IoT segment experienced a 23.7% revenue drop in Q1 2026, explicitly attributed to the reduction (退坡) of national subsidies in mainland China. (截至2026年3月31日止三個月之業績公告, 2026-05-26)
*   **Foreign Exchange Risk:** The Group is exposed to FX risks, primarily related to USD, due to operations in China and India. (截至2025年6月30日止三個月及六個月之業績公告, 2025-08-19)

## Conclusion and Recommendations
Xiaomi has successfully transitioned its EV business from a startup phase to a significant revenue contributor, achieving full-year profitability in 2025. However, the Q1 2026 results signal a shift in the operating environment, characterized by margin pressure from component costs and fierce competition, leading to a return to operating losses for the EV segment. The company's response has been an aggressive capital return strategy, highlighted by the HK$20 billion buyback plan, which signals management confidence but also consumes cash reserves that might be needed for R&D (projected >RMB 200B over 2026-2030) and EV capacity expansion (targeting 550k deliveries in 2026).

**Recommendations for Close Monitoring:**
1.  **Margin Trajectory:** Monitor the gross margins of the Smartphone and EV segments in upcoming quarters to assess if the company can pass on component cost increases or if further profitability erosion is imminent.
2.  **EV Segment Profitability:** Track the operating results of the EV segment to see if the Q1 2026 loss was a transient dip due to product mix (lower SU7 Ultra proportion) or a structural issue related to pricing power and subsidies.
3.  **Buyback Execution vs. Cash Position:** While the buyback program is substantial, monitor the cash flow statements to ensure that the aggressive capital return does not constrain liquidity for R&D and capital expenditure, especially given the significant cash burn in the EV business.
4.  **India Dispute Resolution:** Continue to monitor developments regarding the seized funds in India, as an adverse outcome could materially impact the financial statements (currently unprovisioned).
5.  **VIE Structure Stability:** Ensure the newly restructured VIE arrangements function effectively without administrative friction following the exit of key co-founders from the shareholder registry.
